Focus of the study:

The focus of this meta-analysis is to assess the effectiveness of digital interventions (e.g., wearable, apps, websites) in encouraging physical activity by comparing outcomes between low and high socioeconomic status groups.

It also takes a look at whether the number or kind of behaviour change techniques (BCTS) that are used in these interventions influenced their effectiveness.

Target group:

  • Lower socioeconomic status populations: Determined by income, level of education, employment, or deprivation indices
  • Higher socioeconomic status populations: Used as a comparison group to evaluate fairness in the effects of the interventions

Average effect size:

Negligible, non-significant effect in lower socioeconomic status groups (SMD: 0.06, 95% CI: -0.08 to 0.2), small to moderate, statistically significant effect in higher socioeconomic status groups (SMD: 0.34, 95%CI: 0.22 to 0.45)

Additional results:

  • Digital interventions possible widening the gap in physical activity, seemingly only possible benefiting individuals of higher socioeconomic status
  • Low heterogeneity for both groups, implying relatively consistent results across studies
